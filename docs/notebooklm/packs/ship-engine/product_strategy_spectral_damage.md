TRENCH deliberately targets the filter space nobody else covers. E-mu atlas analysis shows LPFs (types 0-8) dominate 80%+ of preset usage — that's commodity. Types 65, 66, 132+ (phaser, vowel, multi-pole character) are heavily used in real presets but have zero behavioral models anywhere.

**Why:** The boxy region (200-600Hz) is a dead zone in most plugins. Shaped notches that move under modulation — "material stress" — is something you can't get from preset banks or RBJ cookbook filters. Zero-forcing with independent pole/zero placement creates shapes that are genuinely new.

**How to apply:** Forge scoring should reward complexity, spectral damage, shaped notches in low-mids, and high-frequency complexity. Don't optimize for clean LPF sweeps. Types 0-8 are reference material for pipeline validation. Types 65+ are where TRENCH lives. The coverage gap is the target, not a weakness.
