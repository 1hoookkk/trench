TRENCH is a **Material Stress Simulator**, not an accurate hardware emulation. Accuracy is a commodity. TRENCH weaponizes mathematical dishonesty for biological authenticity.

**Four moats:**

1. **Split-Code Law** — poles get stabilized frequency, aux words get raw intent. Filter is internally stressed. Creates "Nyquist Scar" — HF weight from a system pushed into its own architectural limits. No clean-math EQ replicates this.

2. **Asynchronous Pole-Crossing** — Stage-Skewed Pursuit. 6 biquad stages are independent actors (Jaw, Tongue, Throat). Morph makes stages race each other → Spectral Friction. Source of the "liquid" sound — a body reconfiguring its internal organs in real-time.

3. **Hyperbolic Geography** — 2D surface via bilinear interpolation across 4 independent corners (Hyperbolic Paraboloid). Interior contains spectral combinations that never existed on original 1D hardware. Exploring accidental math.

4. **Instrument-Grade PCB Hardware** — Dark gunmetal chassis, gold/copper PCB trace art, iridescent refraction, milled recesses. $4,000 specialized hardware that refuses to be friendly. Not a dark mode app — machined equipment.

**Kill line:** Every other plugin is a Corrective Utility. TRENCH is a Performative Instrument. Producers use it to **author a material**, not fix a mix. Audio as physical substance — stressed, skewed, morphed until it breaks.

**Why:** This frames all product decisions. The moat is the architecture, not the accuracy. Forge output doesn't need to match P2K — it needs to be *stressed material* that couldn't exist anywhere else.

**How to apply:** When making design/feature/sonic decisions, ask "does this make TRENCH more of a stress simulator or more of a clean utility?" Always choose stress.
