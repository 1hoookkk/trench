## 2 Pole Lowpass (P2k_001, filterType=1)

### What It Is
The name is misleading. This is not a simple lowpass; it is a collapsing mouth-and-metal filter that can either hold a bright steel lid over the sound or drag that energy down into a speakable throat. At low Q it is mostly upper-spectrum pressure with one low-mid support point. Raising Q pulls several stages into the 1.2-3.5 kHz vocal corridor while Stage 6 drops to 113-176 Hz and turns the whole shape into something chesty and bodily. A producer would reach for this when they want a synth or drum loop to sound like it is being forced through a resonant cavity rather than merely darkened.

### Stage Roles
- Stage 1: air at 5.9-14.6 kHz. Both. Stress.
- Stage 2: bridge at 1.2-10.3 kHz, spanning chest-adjacent throat up to air-band edge. Both. Identity.
- Stage 3: formant at 1.6-16.3 kHz, landing hardest in EE/EH to singer's-formant territory. Both. Identity.
- Stage 4: bite at 4.5-16.5 kHz. Both. Stress.
- Stage 5: cavity at 2.9-12.8 kHz, from singer's-formant/ear-canal zone to air. Both. Cavity.
- Stage 6: anchor at 113-1295 Hz, from chest resonance to OO/AH throat floor. Both. Anchor.

### Motion Law
Q is the main reconfiguration axis. At both morph extremes it pulls the centroid down by roughly 1.2-1.5 kHz, deepens the nulls by 31-46 dB, and drops four stages from the air/sibilance band into a stacked 1.2-6.0 kHz mouth. Morph then decides how violent that mouth feels. At Q0, morph does not open the filter; it swaps one topology for another, from a mostly high-band blade cluster with a lone 1.3 kHz support point into a much more unstable split where Stage 3 falls to 2.2 kHz, Stage 2 widens to 5.4 kHz, and three stages still hang overhead at 12.8-16.5 kHz. At Q100 the structure becomes more coherent: Stages 2-5 bunch into a tight 1.6-5.6 kHz vocal-bite ladder while Stage 6 stays down in the chest. The most violent single transition is M0_Q0 to M100_Q0, where the peak jumps to 20.6 dB and the filter stops being a bright pressure canopy and becomes a split jaw with a deep throat under it. It feels physical because the chest anchor, mouth formants, and metallic lid move as one anatomy, not as independent EQ bands.

### What Would Break It
If Stage 6 weakened, the filter would lose the body that makes the high cluster feel mounted to a chest cavity. The whole response would read as metallic combing instead of a resonant object. Stage 6 is the load-bearing piece.

### Relatives
Among the analyzed skins, P2k_000 is the closest relative in overall pressure-and-null behavior, but P2k_001 is more bodily because it keeps a true chest anchor below the moving mouth. P2k_000 is the opposite in emphasis: more shredded air, less torso.
