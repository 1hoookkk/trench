## Vocal Ah-Ay-Ee (P2k_016, filterType=16)

### What It Is
This one earns its name only in the middle of its body. At low morph it behaves like a voiced cavity with a soft 500 Hz throat floor and a movable 1.3-2.3 kHz mouth, so you hear the promised vowel drift. But push morph and Q together and the mouth evacuates upward into a harsh 8-14 kHz roof while a lone 380-430 Hz chest murmur stays behind. A producer would reach for it when a sound needs to start as speech and then tear into bright enamel: talking at one corner, fracture at another.

### Stage Roles
- Stage 1: air lid at 9.4-17.7 kHz. Both. Stress.
- Stage 2: formant/bridge at 530 Hz throat floor, 1.34 kHz mouth, then bypass, then 8.49 kHz roof. Both. Bridge.
- Stage 3: formant at 479 Hz to 2.34 kHz, then 14.45 kHz shard band. Both. Identity.
- Stage 4: bite bridge at 2.03-6.37 kHz, then 10.57 kHz crown. Both. Bridge.
- Stage 5: bridge from 1.14 kHz chest-mouth link to 4.18 kHz presence, then 11.55-13.06 kHz air plate. Both. Cavity.
- Stage 6: anchor at 357-435 Hz chest/throat floor, briefly rising to 1.57 kHz at low morph/low Q. Both. Anchor.

### Motion Law
The real law is not a smooth vowel sweep. Morph and Q trade custody of the mouth. At `M0_Q0`, Stages 2 and 3 sit together around 480-530 Hz while Stage 6 rises to 1.57 kHz, so the throat is low and the mouth is narrow. Raising Q at low morph flips that: Stage 6 drops to 357 Hz, Stage 2 climbs to 1.34 kHz, and Stage 3 jumps to 2.34 kHz, creating the clearest Ah-to-Ay-to-Ee spacing in the surface. High morph at low Q is the most natural vocal corner: Stage 6 anchors at 434 Hz while Stages 5, 3, and 4 ladder through 1.14, 1.40, and 2.03 kHz like chest, mouth, and upper mouth. The violent break is `M100_Q0 -> M100_Q100`. Stage 2 wakes from bypass straight into an 8.5 kHz roof, Stage 3 leaves 1.4 kHz for 14.4 kHz, Stages 4 and 5 follow into a 10-12 kHz crown, peak level jumps nearly 20 dB, and notch depth collapses by almost 60 dB. That is not pronunciation; it is the vocal tract turning inside out. It feels physical because the floor stays put while the face peels upward.

### What Would Break It
Stage 6 is load-bearing. Without that 357-435 Hz chest floor, the other stages stop reading as a mouth and become a disconnected bright comb.

### Relatives
Closest to `P2k_013` because both build speech from a low anchor plus a 1-3 kHz mouth ladder, but `P2k_016` is less stable and far more willing to eject its formants into the air band. Opposite of `P2k_015`, which keeps its identity in a rigid metallic grate; this one begins as anatomy and only becomes metal when overstressed.
