## Swept EQ 3/1 Octave (P2k_011, filterType=11)

### What It Is
This is not a normal swept EQ. It behaves like a subtractive mouthpiece that keeps changing which part is flesh and which part is metal: one corner is a buried throat under extreme upper-air cuts, another is a spoken mask, and the hottest state throws that mouth upward into brittle glass while one low bone stays behind. A producer would reach for it when a sound needs to pivot from hollow body to hard surgical glare.

### Stage Roles
- Stage 1: roof switch from 79 Hz sub-floor to 9.4-12.8 kHz air lid. Both. Stress.
- Stage 2: load-bearing anchor from 357-445 Hz throat/body, except one low-Q hidden-roof state at 17.7 kHz. Both. Anchor.
- Stage 3: formant carrier from 479 Hz UH-schwa floor through 1.16-2.34 kHz mouth, then to 10.5 kHz glass. Both. Identity.
- Stage 4: bridge from 1.39 kHz throat-mouth to 4.18 kHz presence, or detached 11.5-13.1 kHz shell. Both. Bridge.
- Stage 5: bite ridge from 2.05 kHz upper mouth to 6.37 kHz sibilance, or 8.5-15.7 kHz metal roof. Both. Stress.
- Stage 6: cavity hinge from DC/null behavior to 1.34 kHz nasal throat, then 14.7 kHz fracture. Both. Cavity.

### Motion Law
At low Q, morph does the strange move: Stage 1 rises from a buried 79 Hz floor to a 9.9 kHz roof while Stages 4 and 5 fall out of the air band and land at 1.39 and 2.05 kHz. The filter does not open smoothly; it swaps skeletons. At `M0`, raising Q assembles a mouth ladder: Stage 2 drops to 357 Hz, Stage 6 appears at 1.34 kHz, then Stages 3, 4, and 5 stack at 2.34, 4.18, and 6.37 kHz under a 9.4 kHz lid. At `M100`, Q does the opposite reconfiguration: the 445/1156/1394/2053 Hz body blows apart into one 374 Hz bone plus an 8.5-14.7 kHz shard cluster. The most violent measured transition is `M0_Q0 -> M0_Q100`, where the centroid jumps about 2.1 kHz and the notch depth relaxes roughly 76 dB, meaning a buried cavity suddenly becomes an articulated face. It feels physical because the throat, mouth, and roof keep trading jobs instead of acting like EQ bands.

### What Would Break It
Stage 2 is the load-bearing piece. Once the filter starts speaking, that 357-445 Hz throat anchor is the only stable body reference left. Remove it and the whole shape turns into floating cuts and bright shards with no chest or jaw underneath.

### Relatives
Closest to P2k_010 and P2k_009: all three are misnamed swept-EQ bodies that keep one low anchor while the upper structure reconfigures into a brighter shell. P2k_011 is the most surgical and binary of the set because it openly swaps roof and mouth roles instead of just lifting a visor. Opposite of P2k_008, which folds an air shell downward into speech; this one can eject speech upward until only a low bone remains.
