## 4 Pole Highpass (P2k_005, filterType=5)

### What It Is
This is not behaving like a simple 4-pole highpass. It starts as a split body with one chest murmur, one low mouth pocket, and three hard blades above 4.5 kHz; morph then packs that anatomy into a much more vocal shell, while Q decides whether the shell speaks from the throat or hisses from the teeth. The giveaway is the extreme M100_Q0 state: 19.8 dB peak, 136 dB range, centroid falling to 1.25 kHz. A producer would reach for this when a sound needs to go from skeletal comb-metal into an articulated mouth with pressure behind it.

### Stage Roles
- Stage 1: bite/air at 4.6-13.0 kHz. Moves with Q. Stress.
- Stage 2: anchor-to-bite at 201 Hz to 5.8 kHz. Both. Identity.
- Stage 3: bridge from 882 Hz throat to 4.0 kHz presence. Both. Identity.
- Stage 4: air blade at 9.4-13.2 kHz or upper-formant carrier at 2.35 kHz. Both. Stress.
- Stage 5: formant bridge at 871 Hz to 3.7 kHz. Both. Cavity.
- Stage 6: formant/anchor at 925 Hz to 5.9 kHz. Both. Anchor.

### Motion Law
Morph is the violent axis at low Q. It drags Stage 2 up from a 201 Hz chest murmur to 1.5 kHz, pulls Stage 6 down from a 2.3 kHz upper-mouth pole to 925 Hz, drops Stage 5 from 1.6 kHz to 871 Hz, and yanks Stage 4 out of the air band into a 2.35 kHz vocal carrier. That is why M100_Q0 explodes into the deepest notch field and biggest peak: the shell closes into a real mouth. Q does the opposite job. At M0 it lifts Stage 2, 3, and 6 upward into a 3.6-5.9 kHz bite ladder while Stage 5 sinks from 1.6 kHz to 1.0 kHz, so the body thins and the teeth harden. At M100, Q partially reopens the shell: Stages 3 and 5 climb together to 3.5-3.7 kHz, Stage 2 jumps to 5.8 kHz, Stage 4 flies back to 10.2 kHz, but Stage 6 refuses to leave the 1.0 kHz throat floor. The most violent single transition is M0_Q0 to M100_Q0. That is where a loose comb becomes a pressurized vocal cavity. It feels physical because one pole stays in the throat while the others either seal the mouth around it or peel away into sibilant metal.

### What Would Break It
If Stage 6 weakened, the filter would lose the throat floor that makes the morph-closed state feel embodied. Without that 925-1025 Hz anchor, the rest of the structure reads as clever combing and presence spikes, not a mouth under pressure. Stage 6 is the load-bearing piece.

### Relatives
Closest to P2k_002 in the way Q can evacuate a voiced cavity and leave a brighter tooth ladder behind, but P2k_005 is more unstable because morph creates a far deeper shell collapse. P2k_004 is its harsher opposite: more blade-first, less mouth-first.
