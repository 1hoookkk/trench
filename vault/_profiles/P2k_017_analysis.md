## Vocal Oo-Ah (P2k_017, filterType=17)

### What It Is
The name only fits one corner. At low Q this can read as a hollow, rounded mouth: a 385-500 Hz throat floor against an 890-913 Hz upper cavity, like an `oo` tube with the lips half closed. But Q does not open that into a broad `ah`. It blows the whole mouth upward into a hard 5-15 kHz roof while the low throat barely survives underneath. A producer would reach for this when a sound needs to start as a muffled vocal chamber and then flash into enamel, breath, and blade.

### Stage Roles
- Stage 1: air lid at 11.4-12.2 kHz. Mostly Q. Stress.
- Stage 2: throat floor / cavity gate at 385-496 Hz, or a 485 Hz anti-resonant choke when Q is low. Mostly Q. Anchor.
- Stage 3: formant hinge from 892-913 Hz upper throat to 8.6-9.6 kHz roof. Both. Identity.
- Stage 4: extreme roof plate at 12.8-15.8 kHz. Mostly Q. Air.
- Stage 5: bridge from 3.7-4.4 kHz presence bite to 5.5-10.6 kHz sibilant crown. Both. Bridge.
- Stage 6: brittle cavity blade at 10.2-15.1 kHz with unit-circle notch forcing. Both. Cavity.

### Motion Law
The law is vertical eviction. Low Q keeps the only believable vocal body: Stage 2 holds the throat around nasal `n` / schwa floor, Stage 3 sits near the `aw` / `oo` upper cavity zone, and Stage 5 adds a 3.7-4.4 kHz teeth edge. Morph at low Q mostly darkens that body by pulling the centroid down from 3.1 kHz to 1.2 kHz; it does not pronounce a new vowel so much as close the mouth and sink the head. Q is the violent control. It activates Stage 2 as a real low pole, then launches Stages 3, 5, and 6 upward together into the sibilance and air bands while peak level jumps 16-25 dB and the deep notches soften. The sharpest break is `M0_Q0 -> M0_Q100`: the 913 Hz mouth hinge becomes an 8.6 kHz roof and the 4.4 kHz bite becomes a 10.6 kHz crown. It feels physical because one throat bone stays low while the rest of the face peels off the skull.

### What Would Break It
Stage 2 is load-bearing. Remove that 385-496 Hz throat floor and the filter stops reading as anatomy at all; it becomes a bright shard lattice with no mouth behind it.

### Relatives
Closest to `P2k_016` because both promise vowels but reserve their strongest motion for a high-band rupture. `P2k_017` is less articulate in the speech zone and more dependent on one low throat anchor. Opposite of `P2k_013`, which keeps its talking body intact under stress instead of evacuating it into the roof.
