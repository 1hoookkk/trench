## Dual EQ + LP Morph (P2k_019, filterType=19)

### What It Is
This one partly earns its name, but only if you hear the lowpass as a moving brow, not the whole instrument. The core sound is a split face: a low chest/throat anchor, a mid vocal mask, and an upper lid that can hover above the mask or clamp down onto it. A producer would use this to make a tone feel spoken, boxed-in, and pressurized, then smear that speech into a darker hood without losing the bite.

### Stage Roles
- Stage 1: lowpass lid / upper brow at 2.7-7.7 kHz. Both. Stress.
- Stage 2: chest-to-mouth bridge at 199 Hz to 1.59 kHz. Mostly Q. Bridge.
- Stage 3: throat cavity at 351 Hz, then upper-mouth / bite lift to 6.5 kHz. Both. Cavity.
- Stage 4: air-to-presence cap at 4.4-10.6 kHz. Mostly Q. Air.
- Stage 5: formant mask at 1.48-5.27 kHz. Mostly Q. Identity.
- Stage 6: bite bridge at 3.25-5.06 kHz. Both. Bridge.

### Motion Law
The law is convergence under pressure. At `M0_Q0`, the anatomy is split wide apart: Stage 2 is a 199 Hz chest floor, Stage 3 sits near a 1.0 kHz mouth hinge, Stage 5 and Stage 6 form a 4.8-5.3 kHz bite shelf, and Stage 4 hangs alone near 10 kHz like a detached lid. Raising Q at low morph pulls the whole structure inward. Stage 1 drops from 4.9 kHz to 2.7 kHz, Stage 4 falls from 10 kHz to 4.4 kHz, Stage 5 collapses from 5.3 kHz to 1.5 kHz, and Stage 3 dives to a 351 Hz throat cavity. The result is a face assembling itself under a hood. Morph then decides whether that hood stays coherent. At low Q, morph spreads the body upward and loosens the carve. At high Q, morph lifts Stage 1, Stage 4, and Stage 5 back toward 3.3-6.2 kHz while Stage 2 stays parked around 1.5 kHz, so the low throat survives and the mask hardens into a narrower plate. The most violent break is `M0_Q0 -> M0_Q100`, where the 1.0 kHz hinge becomes a 351 Hz cavity and the 5.3 kHz mask slams into 1.5 kHz. It feels physical because the filter behaves like jaw, mask, and brow tightening against each other rather than six independent bumps.

### What Would Break It
Stage 5 is load-bearing. Remove that 1.5-5.3 kHz mask and the filter loses the thing that makes the chest, throat, and lid read as one face instead of scattered shelving.

### Relatives
Closest to `P2k_018`, but this one is darker and more unified because the lid really clamps onto the face instead of letting the roof fully detach. It also shares `P2k_013`'s habit of building speech from a low anchor plus a mid mask. Opposite of `P2k_015`, which keeps its identity in a bright comb roof rather than a hooded vocal plate.
