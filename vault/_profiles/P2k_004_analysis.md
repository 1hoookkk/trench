## 2 Pole Highpass (P2k_004, filterType=4)

### What It Is
This is not behaving like a simple 2-pole highpass. It starts as an almost bodyless air lattice with one sub-chest murmur underneath, then Q drags several stages down into a throat, mouth, and presence stack while one blade stays overhead. Morph decides whether that anatomy is a buried cavity or a screaming split shell. A producer would use this when a sound needs to go from hollow metal spray into a sharp, talk-like pressure shape.

### Stage Roles
- Stage 1: anchor at 59 Hz to bite at 5.1-5.8 kHz. Both. Anchor.
- Stage 2: bridge at 2.4-10.3 kHz, from AH/OO upper throat into sibilance. Both. Identity.
- Stage 3: formant at 871 Hz to 12.9 kHz, dropping from air into OO/telephone territory with Q. Both. Identity.
- Stage 4: bite at 3.5 kHz or air at 13.6-16.8 kHz. Moves with Q. Stress.
- Stage 5: bridge at 2.4-14.7 kHz, from singer's-formant bite into air. Both. Stress.
- Stage 6: anchor at 456-511 Hz or bite at 2.6-16.3 kHz. Both. Load-bearing anchor.

### Motion Law
This filter is built from opposing moves. At M0, raising Q pulls Stages 3, 4, 5, and 6 out of the 13-16 kHz air band and drops them into an 456 Hz to 3.5 kHz mouth, while Stage 1 rises off the floor into a 5.1 kHz bite point. At M100, Q does a narrower rebuild: Stage 6 still falls into a 511 Hz chest/throat anchor, Stage 3 lands at 2.5 kHz, Stage 2 rises to 4.5 kHz, but Stage 4 refuses to join and stays as a hard 16 kHz blade. Morph is the fracture axis. At Q0 it is violent: Stage 1 explodes from 59 Hz to 13.5 kHz, Stage 6 collapses from 16.3 kHz to 2.6 kHz, and the filter throws a 72.5 dB peak while the centroid drops, so the sound gets heavier even as most poles stay high. At Q100 morph is gentler, mainly spreading the 456 Hz to 3.5 kHz mouth upward into a 511 Hz to 9.6 kHz split jaw. The most violent transition is M0_Q0 to M100_Q0. That is where the buried notch forest turns into a pressurized shell with one huge scream ridge. It feels physical because the chest anchor and the teeth do not rise together; one side of the mouth opens while the other clenches.

### What Would Break It
If Stage 6 weakened, the filter would lose the low throat that makes the high-band blades feel mounted to a body. Without it, the loud morph state becomes anonymous whistle and comb. Stage 6 is the load-bearing piece.

### Relatives
Closest to P2k_003 in the way morph can shatter one topology and Q can rebuild a voiced cavity underneath it, but P2k_004 is harsher and more asymmetrical. P2k_002 is a cleaner opposite: it swaps teeth for throat more neatly, while P2k_004 keeps one hand on the blade.
