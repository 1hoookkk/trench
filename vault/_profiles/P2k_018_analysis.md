## Dual EQ Morph (P2k_018, filterType=18)

### What It Is
This is not a tidy dual-EQ sweeper. At low Q it behaves like a split body: one low throat node around 440-760 Hz, one mouth node around 1.4-1.6 kHz, and a detached glass roof above 6 kHz. Raising Q at low morph pulls that roof down into a dense 1.1-5.4 kHz face ladder, like the filter suddenly grows cheeks, teeth, and a nasal bridge. Then high morph can break that face apart again by firing one stage back into the air band. A producer would use this to turn a plain tone into a speaking, pressurized mask that can still fracture into metal.

### Stage Roles
- Stage 1: bite/air lid at 3.9-14.4 kHz. Mostly Q, with morph spreading the lid wider. Stress.
- Stage 2: anchor at 82-761 Hz. Both. Anchor.
- Stage 3: formant hinge at 1.1-1.6 kHz, with one violent jump to 18.2 kHz. Both. Identity.
- Stage 4: bridge from 1.38-3.14 kHz face zone to a 13.5-16.1 kHz roof plate. Both. Bridge.
- Stage 5: air crown at 5.4-11.7 kHz. Both. Air.
- Stage 6: cavity/bite node at 1.88-2.54 kHz or a 10.1-16.0 kHz blade when Q is low. Both. Cavity.

### Motion Law
The main relationship is roof collapse. At low Q, Stages 1, 4, 5, and 6 are scattered high above the body while Stage 2 and Stage 3 hold the only believable throat and mouth. Raising Q at low morph drags three of those high stages down together into a near-contiguous ladder at 2.5, 3.1, 3.9, and 5.4 kHz, while Stage 2 dives from 443 Hz to 82 Hz. That makes the whole shape close inward and carve much harder: dynamic range explodes from 48.5 dB to 131.4 dB and the centroid drops nearly 900 Hz. Morph then decides whether that new face stays coherent. At high Q, morph keeps the throat low but splits the upper anatomy: Stage 4 and Stage 6 sink toward 1.4-1.9 kHz while Stage 1 rises to 6.5 kHz and Stage 3 detonates from 1.6 kHz to 18.2 kHz. The most violent single transition is that `M100_Q0 -> M100_Q100` Stage 3 launch. It feels physical because the low throat keeps breathing while the mask above it either cinches into a face or tears off into the roof.

### What Would Break It
Stage 2 is load-bearing. Remove that 82-761 Hz throat floor and the rest becomes a clever notch scaffold with no chest, no jaw, and no reason for the upper ladder to read as anatomy.

### Relatives
