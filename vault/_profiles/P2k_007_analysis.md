## 4 Pole Bandpass (P2k_007, filterType=7)

### What It Is
This is not acting like a normal 4-pole bandpass. It is a six-stage ladder that starts as a low throat cluster under one hard presence tooth, then morph and Q keep ratcheting the whole body upward until it becomes a bright speaking mask and finally a glassy upper-mouth scream. The sound is less "bandpass sweep" than pressure climbing through the skull: chest to vowel, vowel to bite, bite to metallic glare. A producer would use this to make a source sound like it is rising from muffled mouth resonance into urgent, cutting articulation.

### Stage Roles
- Stage 1: bite anchor at 935 Hz or presence lid at 3.8-4.0 kHz. Moves with Q. Identity.
- Stage 2: bridge from 548 Hz throat to 4.4 kHz bite. Both. Stress.
- Stage 3: formant bridge from 600 Hz throat to 4.7 kHz bite. Both. Identity.
- Stage 4: formant bridge from 376 Hz chest-mouth to 3.2 kHz presence. Both. Cavity.
- Stage 5: anchor from 322 Hz vocal-tract floor to 2.75 kHz ear-canal bite. Both. Anchor.
- Stage 6: load-bearing carrier from 580 Hz throat to 4.7 kHz metallic edge. Both. Stress.

### Motion Law
Everything except Stage 1 behaves like a geared rack. At low Q, morph lifts Stages 2-6 together from a 322-600 Hz murmur into a 1.27-2.37 kHz vowel body while Stage 1 stays parked at 4.0 kHz, so the filter grows a mouth under a fixed tooth. At M0, raising Q does a gentler version of the same move: the whole lower cluster doubles upward into 688-1171 Hz and Stage 1 drops down to 935 Hz, briefly making the structure most speech-like. At M100, Q becomes the violent move. Stages 2-6 all lunge again into 2.75-4.73 kHz while Stage 1 stays high, so the earlier mouth turns into a clenched presence mask. The harshest transition is M100_Q0 to M100_Q100 because that is where the broad vowel body loses its throat floor and compresses into upper-mid pressure. It feels physical because five stages move like linked bones while one stage acts as the tooth or palate they keep pushing against.

### What Would Break It
If Stage 6 weakened, the filter would lose the carrier that lets the low throat cluster become a coherent mouth and later a metallic face. Stage 5 provides floor, but Stage 6 is the load-bearing piece that keeps the climb from 580 Hz to 4.7 kHz feeling continuous instead of fragmented.

### Relatives
Closest to P2k_005 and P2k_006 because all three build a mouth under a brighter lid, but P2k_007 is more disciplined: it ratchets upward in linked steps instead of splitting into separate body and whistle anatomies. Opposite of P2k_002, which uses Q to trade teeth for throat; P2k_007 mostly keeps climbing until the whole face hardens.
