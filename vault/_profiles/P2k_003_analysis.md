## 6 Pole Lowpass (P2k_003, filterType=3)

### What It Is
This is another dishonest name. It is not a straight 6-pole lowpass; it behaves like a body that flips from a glassy blade crown into a speaking cavity. At low Q, most of the structure lives in the sibilance and air bands while one near-sub anchor keeps the floor alive, so the sound reads as hollow metal with a deep chest murmur underneath. Raising Q pulls that crown down into a 316 Hz to 4.4 kHz mouth-and-throat stack while keeping a hard 7-9 kHz edge above it. A producer would use this to push a source from brittle shell into vocal pressure.

### Stage Roles
- Stage 1: anchor at 59 Hz to 19.5 kHz. Both. Anchor.
- Stage 2: air at 7.4-10.4 kHz. Moves with Q. Stress.
- Stage 3: formant at 871 Hz to 12.9 kHz. Both. Identity.
- Stage 4: bite at 3.5-13.6 kHz. Moves with Q. Stress.
- Stage 5: bridge at 316 Hz to 14.7 kHz. Both. Identity.
- Stage 6: bridge at 456 Hz to 16.3 kHz. Both. Load-bearing identity.

### Motion Law
Morph and Q do different jobs. Morph at Q0 tears the filter open: Stage 1 explodes from 59 Hz to 14.7 kHz, Stage 5 drops from 14.7 kHz to 1.4 kHz, and Stage 6 crashes from 16.3 kHz to a weak 5.4 kHz bridge. That lifts the centroid by 2.4 kHz and strips more than 100 dB of notch depth, so the filter stops being a buried cavity and becomes a brighter split jaw. Q then rebuilds anatomy. At M0, Q pulls Stage 3 to 871 Hz, Stage 5 to 2.36 kHz, and Stage 6 to 456 Hz while leaving Stage 2 and Stage 4 higher, creating a chest-floor plus mouth plus teeth layout. At M100, Q pushes harder: Stage 5 falls to 316 Hz, Stage 6 locks at 2.7 kHz with extreme radius, and Stage 3 takes the 4.4 kHz presence slot while Stage 2/4 stay as a 7.4/9.3 kHz lid. The most violent transition is M0_Q0 to M100_Q0, where the 153 dB notch forest collapses into a far shallower, brighter shape. It feels physical because the floor, mouth, and lid trade responsibility like chest, tongue, and teeth.

### What Would Break It
If Stage 6 weakened, the high-Q states would lose their speaking center. Stage 5 gives the body a floor, but Stage 6 is the load-bearing piece because it becomes the stable 456 Hz to 2.7 kHz bridge that makes the upper blades feel attached to a mouth instead of floating above it.

### Relatives
It is closest to P2k_002 because both let Q rebuild a bright shell into a voiced cavity, but P2k_003 is less binary: morph scrambles the skeleton before Q reassembles it. P2k_001 is a chest-plus-lid cousin, while P2k_000 is the opposite extreme, more air fracture and less body.
