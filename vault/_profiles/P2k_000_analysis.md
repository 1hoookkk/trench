## No Filter (P2k_000, filterType=0)

### What It Is
Despite the name, this is not bypass. It behaves like a brittle air-comb that can suddenly grow a throat and presence ridge when Q comes up. At low Q it is mostly a shredded top-end cavity with deep nulls and very little body; at high Q, morph pulls that fractured haze down into a speakable 0.8-4.4 kHz zone while keeping a hard metallic ceiling above it. A producer would reach for this when they want a sound to feel scraped, hollow, and pressurized rather than simply filtered.

### Stage Roles
- Stage 1: air at 0-16.7 kHz. Both. Stress.
- Stage 2: formant at 0.8-3.5 kHz, crossing OO/ER to presence. Both. Identity.
- Stage 3: bite at 4.4-15.7 kHz, from consonant edge into air band. Both. Stress.
- Stage 4: cavity at 8.0-14.2 kHz in sibilance/air territory. Both. Cavity.
- Stage 5: air at 9.7-18.0 kHz. Both. Cavity.
- Stage 6: bridge at 2.2-16.3 kHz, landing on EE/telephone/presence territory at high Q. Both. Identity.

### Motion Law
The motion is a reconfiguration, not a sweep. At low Q, four stages bunch in the air band and carve a dense notch forest while Stage 2 is the only real mid anchor. Raising Q pulls Stage 2 and Stage 6 into the 0.8-2.2 kHz vowel throat, Stage 3 and Stage 4 lock into a 4.4/8.0 kHz consonant-sibilance ladder, and the whole centroid jumps upward by 1.6-3.3 kHz. Morph at low Q makes the body darker and lower, but morph at high Q does the opposite: it keeps the low-mid throat nearly fixed while the top cluster collapses from 16-18 kHz down toward 9.7-10.7 kHz, which is where the 18.3 dB peak appears. The most violent transition is the diagonal into M100_Q100: that is where the filter stops being an air shredder and becomes a pressurized talking metal mouth. It feels physical because the low throat stages and the high blades do not move independently; they clamp against each other like jaw, tongue, and teeth.

### What Would Break It
Remove Stage 2 and the filter loses its only durable throat/body reference; the whole structure turns into anonymous HF fracture. Stage 2 is the load-bearing piece because every usable state depends on that 0.8-3.5 kHz carrier staying underneath the moving air cluster.

### Relatives
Nearest so far to P2k_012 and P2k_013, which also build identity from a low-mid throat plus upper bite stages, but those filters keep a clearer vocal spine. P2k_000 is harsher and more misleadingly named: less “phaser,” more collapsing air cavity with a sudden mouth at high Q.
