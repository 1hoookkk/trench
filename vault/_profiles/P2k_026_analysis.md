## Millennium (P2k_026, filterType=26)

### What It Is
A metallic comb grille that can be dragged down into a hollow, talking mouth. At the origin it lives almost entirely in the sibilance/air bands (bright, fizzy, “future” sheen); at high morph with low Q it collapses into a full F1→F3 vocal ladder with abyssal cancellations, turning pads/bass into a chewed, pressurized voice-mask rather than a smooth sweep.

### Stage Roles
- Stage 1: air lid at 6.40–14.66 kHz (ultra-bright roof that can drop into sibilance). Both. Stress.
- Stage 2: bite/air spar at 4.93–12.47 kHz (sizzle edge ↔ presence bite). Both. Stress.
- Stage 3: bridge rib at 3.14–10.41 kHz (clarity band ↔ air). Both. Identity.
- Stage 4: mask rung at 1.74–8.43 kHz (nasal/F2 territory ↔ sibilant roof). Both. Identity.
- Stage 5: jaw formant at 0.41–6.30 kHz (F1 floor ↔ sibilance tooth). Both. Anchor.
- Stage 6: throat + vent at 0.37–3.63 kHz, plus an air-notch that sits near ~12.15 kHz or ~17.96 kHz. Both. Cavity.

### Motion Law
- At Q0, Stages 1–5 move as a locked ladder: they all slide down together by *kilohertz* as morph rises (14.7/12.5/10.4/8.4/6.3 kHz → 6.4/4.9/3.1/1.7/0.41 kHz). That single move is the “Millennium” trick: a bright roof grille becomes a full mouth/throat stack, and the response fractures into extreme voids (deepest notches at `M100_Q0`).
- Q is not “more resonance”; it’s a reconfiguration pressure that depends on where you are. At low morph, raising Q clamps the whole chassis downward (centroid drops; notches deepen). At high morph, raising Q vents the comb: Stages 1–5 lift back upward and their poles pull inward (the razor edge softens), while the floor *cross-swaps*—Stage 5 jumps 0.41 → 1.80 kHz as Stage 6 drops 0.98 → 0.37 kHz. It feels like the jaw bone changes hands.
- Most violent transition: `M0_Q0 -> M100_Q0` (morph at low Q), where the structure goes from a bright air grille (only one stage in the presence band) to a stacked vocal tract with near-silent carve-outs.

### What Would Break It
Stage 5. It’s the jaw drop that creates the true F1 floor (and the “mouth exists” illusion). Without it, Millennium stays a bright serrated mask and loses the brutal hollow vocal collapse at high morph/low Q.

### Relatives
Closest to `P2k_025` (comb-mask that changes *anatomy* instead of just “opening”) and `P2k_024` (jaw/void aggression), but Millennium is more top-heavy at the origin: it starts as a pure air-grille and only becomes a mouth when you force morph at low Q. Opposite of `P2k_019`, which reads as a hooded clamp with far less “grill-to-vowel” identity swap.
